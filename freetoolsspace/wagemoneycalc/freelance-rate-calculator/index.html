What this freelance rate calculator result means

This freelance rate calculator turns a target income into a practical minimum hourly rate. It is designed for searches like freelance pay calculator, freelance calculator, freelance rate calculator, and freelance price calculator where the key question is: “What should I charge per hour so I still earn enough after non-billable time and fees?”

The result is not just a wage conversion. Freelancers need to cover unpaid admin work, sales calls, revisions, sick days, vacation, software, payment processing, marketplace commissions, taxes, and slow months. If you price only from a normal employee salary, your freelance hourly rate can be too low.

Freelance hourly rate formula

hourly rate = target annual income ÷ annual billable hours ÷ (1 - platform fee %)

Annual billable hours are calculated from billable hours per week multiplied by working weeks after time off. For example, 25 billable hours per week and 4 weeks off gives 25 × 48 = 1,200 billable hours per year. If the target income is $80,000 and the platform fee is 10%, the minimum rate needs to be higher than $66.67 because part of every invoice is lost to fees.

Freelance pricing examples

Full-time freelancer example

If you want $80,000 per year, can bill 25 hours per week, and take 4 weeks off, you have about 1,200 billable hours. Before fees, that means at least $66.67 per billable hour.

Marketplace fee example

If a platform keeps 10%, divide by 0.90. A $66.67 base rate becomes about $74.07 so the post-fee income still supports your target.

Project price example

For a 12-hour project at a $75 hourly freelance rate, the starting project price is $900 before extra revisions, rush delivery, or licensing costs.

How to choose a safer freelance rate

Use the calculator result as your floor, not your dream rate. A good freelance price also includes skill level, client value, revision risk, urgency, communication time, and the cost of finding the next client. For low-risk recurring work, you may accept a rate near the minimum. For urgent, strategic, or highly specialized work, charge above the minimum.

If your GSC query is “freelance pay calculator,” the page should answer both income and pricing intent. Start with the annual income you want, then test different billable-hour scenarios. If you only bill 15–20 hours per week, your needed hourly rate can be much higher than expected.

FAQ

How do I calculate my freelance hourly rate?

Start with target annual income, divide by annual billable hours after time off, then adjust upward for platform fees, taxes, expenses, and profit margin.

Why is a freelance rate higher than an employee hourly wage?

Freelancers must cover unpaid admin time, client acquisition, software, taxes, vacation, sick days, payment fees, and gaps between projects.

Can I use this as a freelance project price calculator?

Yes. Multiply the calculated hourly rate by estimated project hours, then add extra margin for revisions, rush work, licensing, or scope risk.
