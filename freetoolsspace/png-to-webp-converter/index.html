PNG to WebP conversion notes

PNG is reliable for transparency and sharp screenshots, but WebP often gives a smaller file for web publishing. This converter lets you choose quality and optionally resize large images. If your PNG uses transparency, leave the background as transparent. If a website requires a solid background, enter a color such as white before converting.

When WebP helps

WebP is useful for blog graphics, UI assets, product images, and lightweight landing pages. Always keep your original PNG file if you may need to edit it later.

For best performance, test the converted WebP on your target browser or CMS. Some older systems may still require PNG or JPG uploads.

FAQ

Why convert PNG to WebP?

WebP often creates smaller files than PNG, which can improve page speed and reduce bandwidth.

Does WebP support transparency?

Yes, WebP supports transparency in modern browsers. Keep the background set to transparent when you need it.

Is the conversion local?

The converter uses browser canvas export, so normal conversion happens locally on your device.

When should I keep PNG instead?

Keep PNG when you need maximum compatibility, pixel-perfect screenshots, or a workflow that does not accept WebP.
