About this free online tool

This free color contrast checker helps designers, developers, and content teams test whether text is readable against a background. It reports the contrast ratio and common WCAG pass levels for normal and large text.

Accessible color choices improve usability for people with low vision, bright screens, poor lighting, and color perception differences. Use the result while designing buttons, cards, headings, banners, and body copy.

The calculation runs instantly in your browser. For full accessibility compliance, combine contrast checks with keyboard testing, semantic HTML, labels, and real user review.

FAQ

What ratio passes WCAG AA?

Normal text generally needs 4.5:1. Large text generally needs 3:1.

What ratio passes WCAG AAA?

Normal text generally needs 7:1. Large text generally needs 4.5:1.

Can I use hex colors?

Yes. Enter colors such as #1f2937 and #ffffff.

Does contrast guarantee accessibility?

No. It is one important check, but accessible design also needs structure, labels, focus states, and usable interactions.
