What this tool does

The Keyword Density Checker helps writers, marketers, and site owners review SEO copy before publishing. Paste a draft, enter the target keyword, and see total word count, exact keyword matches, density percentage, and the most repeated single-word terms.

Use the result as a quality check, not as a mechanical ranking recipe. Healthy SEO copy usually answers the search intent clearly, uses related language naturally, and avoids repeating the same phrase so often that the page feels forced. This browser-based checker gives you a quick signal when a landing page, product description, or article draft may need more context, fewer repetitions, or clearer topical focus.

How to use keyword density without over-optimizing

Start by checking whether the main phrase appears in important places such as the title, introduction, one subheading, and a helpful conclusion. Then look at the percentage in context. A short page with two mentions can show a high density, while a long guide may need several natural mentions to stay focused.

If one phrase dominates the output, rewrite nearby sentences with synonyms, examples, and user questions. For SEO pages on FreeToolsSpace, this pairs well with the Meta Description Generator for search snippets and the Open Graph Generator for share previews.

Formula or rule

keyword density = target keyword occurrences divided by total word count, multiplied by 100

Practical SEO checklist

After checking density, confirm that the page also has a unique title tag, a clear meta description, one descriptive H1, crawlable internal links, and a canonical URL. Keyword balance helps, but it works best together with useful content and solid technical SEO.
